Sonic Light 2003, Amsterdam

What is visual music?

This remarkable festival and conference answered this question in many ways, bringing together diverse practitioners, including artists, composers, and other experts in the field.

There was a fascinating collection of films showing works of early experimental film makers. The conference presented the ideas of current practitioners, and a series of concerts and installations presented the work.

I presented my HyperSphere, with a new much enhanced 3D projection. I wrote the software while in Thailand. It uses OpenGL to create virtual forms with images taken in Thailand, and transforms them into an abstract flow. I call the software Cubism because it re-explores the process of using images from the everyday world and 'abstracts' them. I chose to use a square, or large number of squares, which I textured with the images. Initially I stepped outside the room I was working in and took some pictures of a mango tree growing there. Later I added images from other parts of Thailand. Ancient ruins, statues of the Buddha, waterfalls, an old gateway, a ride on a ferry at dusk across the Bangkok river, a Karen tribal house and much more. I chose images that had interesting textures, colours and contrasts.

I constructed a method to move the squares in a random walk within a cubic lattice. Later I added a tessalated surface which I could undulate, but still all made from squares. The program generates animation in real time at high resolution, producing an hour long show, which then replays with variations generated by the computer. This new work was shown together with a version of The Wave Equation.
My installation was located at The Paradiso, a famous night spot in Amsterdam. This was also the venue for the concerts and video screenings. I gave a lecture at the conference. A book will be published of the conference, including transcripts of all the talks.
